'Curriculum Overview'

The school offers a diverse curriculum to provide a well-rounded education for students. Here's a brief overview of the key subjects:

Mathematics

The primary objectives of the math curriculum are to ensure a grasp of mathematical concepts and foster the growth of effective problem solving skills. The curriculum encompasses areas such as algebra, geometry, calculus and statistics.

Science

The science curriculum covers a range of scientific disciplines such as physics, chemistry and biology. Students actively participate in hands on experiments and interactive activities to cultivate a passion, for exploration.

History

History classes cover a range of historical eras and events focusing on analyzing historical sources and developing critical thinking skills. Students gain an understanding of global cultural and political developments.

English

The primary objective of the curriculum is to enhance language skills in reading, writing and oral communication. When students engage with literature, they have the opportunity to explore timeless well as contemporary works fostering a passion, for language and self expression.

Filipino

Filipino classes have a curriculum that focuses on enhancing students language skills in reading, writing, speaking and listening. The lessons aim to develop an understanding and appreciation of the Filipino language through activities such, as analyzing literature exploring culture and practicing practical communication.

Assessment and Feedback

In our opinion, continuous evaluation is the effective method to track the progress of students. Assessments come in forms such as exams, projects, active participation in class and regular quizzes. This comprehensive approach enables us to accurately identify each students strengths and areas for improvement.

After assessments, students receive feedback that emphasizes suggestions for enhancement and constructive criticism. We encourage communication among teachers, students and parents/guardians to foster a supportive educational environment.

Biology 1

The digestive system is truly remarkable with its processes that coordinate the breakdown of food and absorption of essential nutrients. This is particularly fascinating in the field of biology. This intricate system, consisting of the stomach, small intestine and liver collaborates harmoniously to convert food into energy and vital components. Students who delve into this subject explore the captivating interaction between anatomy and physiology from the movements in the stomach to enzymatic processes facilitated by organs, like the pancreas. Through hands on activities, studies and dissections, students gain an understanding of the topic. It showcases how different aspects of digestion come together seamlessly.

English 3

In Breshiana School, English classes students explore poetic forms, including Shakespearean and Petrarchan sonnets. They delve into the characteristics of Petrarchan sonnets, which typically present a problem in the octave and offer a resolution in the sestet. These sonnets often revolve around themes of love. On the hand, Shakespearean sonnets provide a platform for expressing a wide range of emotions from love to contemplation of life's complexities. This is made possible by their structure of three quatrains followed by a rhymed couplet. Through analysis and creative exercises students gain an understanding of the intricate artistic qualities inherent in these longstanding English poetry traditions as well, as appreciate their structural intricacies.
